Imagine receiving an official-looking court notice or a landlord eviction notice. For most people, the immediate reaction is panic, confusion, and anxiety, because legal language is incredibly hard to understand and hire a lawyer is expensive and slow.

To solve this, I built the Legal First-Aid Agent—an intelligent, secure legal notice assistant designed for citizens in India. Belonging to the Agents for Good track, this agent helps everyday people parse complex notices, understand their rights and key deadlines, and draft formal response letters safely.

[1:00 — ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let's look at the system architecture. When a user uploads a notice or enters a query, it first passes through our Security Checkpoint. If it's safe, the Orchestrator takes over. It sends the notice to the Document Reader Agent which extracts structured facts like the sender, recipient, and notice type. Then, the Orchestrator delegates to two specialized sub-agents in parallel: the Rights Explainer Agent—which uses our Indian Kanoon and Google Search MCP servers to find relevant laws and judgments—and the Deadline Tracker Agent, which calculates target reply dates using our local legal MCP tools. Once analyzed, the system pauses for Human-in-the-Loop review to collect the user's specific statements. Finally, the Response Drafter Agent generates a professional reply letter and plain-language summary, before the Final Output node appends a mandatory legal disclaimer.

[3:00 — SECURITY & MCP]
A key feature of this application is privacy and safety. Our Security Checkpoint strips out personal identifiers like Aadhar card numbers and phone numbers in memory before the LLM is queried. It also immediately rejects prompt injection attempts and logs events. On the data side, the Indian Kanoon and Google Search MCP tools fetch accurate legal databases dynamically.
